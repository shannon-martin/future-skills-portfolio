The dataset [‘Smoking prevalence in young people (SALSUS)’](https://opendata.scot/datasets/scottish+government-smoking+prevalence+in+young+people+%2528salsus%2529/) was obtained from Open Data Scotland.
This data is licenced under [UK Open Government Licence v3.0](https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/doc/open-government-licence/version/3/).

Visualising this data showed that there was a steady increase over time for all age groups (13, 15, and All) that report having never smoked. Around 87% of 13-year-olds and 70% of 15-year-olds report never having smoked in 2018, compared to around 57% of 13-year-olds and 41% of 15-year-olds from 2002. There was a considerable decrease in the number of 15-year-olds that reported smoking regularly and having tried smoking once. In 2002, females also had higher reported rates of smoking compared to males, though this disparity appears to have narrowed in 2018.

I used a PivotTable to average the values by year, which allowed me to create a PivotChart showing the trend of smoking behaviour over time. I also added slicers to control the filtering by Age Group/Gender to explore the data selectively.
